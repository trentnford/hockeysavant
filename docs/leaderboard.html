Leaderboard

Every qualified player, sortable by overall group percentiles or any individual stat. Click a column header to sort.

How to read it. Every number is a percentile (0–100) ranked within the chosen pool — forwards vs. forwards, defensemen vs. defensemen, goalies vs. goalies — so blue is cold and red is elite. OVR is itself a percentile: each player's group scores are blended with the same weights their trophy uses (Hart for forwards — offense-led, so sheltered defensive minutes don't dominate; Norris for defensemen; Vezina for goalies). That skill composite is then mixed 75/25 with workload — per-game ice time (min/GP) for skaters, total ice time for goalies — and re-ranked across the pool. Group columns are bold; the rest are the individual stats behind them.

Grey goalie cells (Shot Quality Faced, Shots Faced/60) are workload context, neither good nor bad. Some stats are better when low (xGoals Against, Giveaways, Rebounds/Shot); their percentile is already flipped so high is always good. Qualifiers: skaters need >200 minutes or >20 games; goalies need >15 games or >600 minutes.
