Glossary & Methodology

What the numbers mean and how they're ranked.

Everything is a percentile

Each stat is shown as a percentile from 0 to 100 — the share of peers a player beats. 90 means better than 90% of comparable players that season. Color runs cold (low) to elite (high); for stats where less is better, the percentile is flipped so high is always good.

Ranked within position

Percentiles are pooled by position: forwards vs. forwards, defensemen vs. defensemen, goalies vs. goalies, recomputed each season. A defenseman at the 90th percentile in offense is elite among defensemen, not against forwards.

All vs. 5-on-5

All situations include power play and penalty kill; 5-on-5 isolates even-strength play, stripping out special-teams role. Selke (defensive forward) is judged on 5-on-5 so penalty-killers aren't punished for the chances the PK concedes.

OVR & the awards

A player's OVR blends their group percentiles with the same weights their trophy uses — Hart for forwards (offense-led), Norris for defensemen, Vezina for goalies — then that composite is re-ranked into its own percentile.

Who qualifies

Skaters need more than 200 minutes or 20 games; goalies need more than 15 games or 600 minutes. Awards also fold in a workload factor so a hot small sample can't out-rank a full season.

A caveat on defense

The on-ice “against” stats (xGoals Against/60) reward sheltered usage — a bottom-six player who faces weak competition can post strong suppression. It's the best team-defense signal here, not a pure individual-skill measure.
