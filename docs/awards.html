How these are picked. Each trophy is scored from the season's percentile profiles — the same Offense / Two-Way / Defense (and goalie) group scores shown on a player card — combined with simple, fixed weights, then blended 75/25 with a workload factor — per-game ice time (min/GP) for skaters, total ice time for goalies — so a lightly-used player can't out-rank a true workhorse. They are the model's view, not the real voters'.

Rookies (Calder) are approximated as players in their first qualifying season within this dataset (2008–09 onward), so it is unavailable for 2008–09 and can misflag late-arriving veterans.

Cross-position & the MVP. Most numbers on a player card are ranked within position, so a defenseman at the 99th percentile in offense is elite among defensemen, not vs. forwards. The Hart is the exception: it scores the whole league — forwards, defensemen and goalies — each on their own role, with skaters compared on one all-skater scale. On that honest footing forwards usually out-value defensemen, so D rarely crack the top five, and a dominant goalie can win outright.
